What is Open Science?

There is no single definition of “open science” that covers all practices and principles, but the Open Science Training Handbook offers a useful starting point.
Open Science is the practice of science in such a way that others can collaborate and contribute, where research data, lab notes and other research processes are freely available, under terms that enable reuse, redistribution and reproduction of the research and its underlying data and methods.
Examples of Open Science practices
Preregistered studies, sharing research data and code, open peer review, and ensuring free access to published work are all examples of open science practices.
Open Science as a default
In OSCN, we do not distinguish “open” science from “traditional” science — often summarized as “Open science is just science done right”. Modern technological and societal developments have changed how science is done, but academic systems are still adapting to fully use these possibilities.
