ABOUT OSCN

Open Science Community Nijmegen

The Open Science Community Nijmegen (OSCN) is a bottom-up network of researchers, students, and professionals who are committed to making research more transparent, reproducible, and collaborative. Guided by values of openness, fairness, diversity, and inclusivity, we aim to foster a more open research culture by connecting people and sharing knowledge.

The people behind OSCN

Organizing Team

OSCN is coordinated by a multidisciplinary team of researchers, students, and professional staff who are committed to advancing Open Science within and beyond Nijmegen. Together, they organize events, facilitate knowledge sharing, and support a more transparent and collaborative research culture.

Anita Eerland

Behavioural Science Institute (BSI), Radboud University

I work as an associate professor of Communication Science – and Open Science Officer – within the Behavioural Science Institute (BSI). I joined the Radboud University and OSCN in 2021 after working at Utrecht University and founding the first Dutch Open Science Community there together with Loek Brinkman in 2018. I am a board member of the network of Open Science Communities the Netherlands (OSC-NL) and the International Network of Open Science and Scholarship Communities (INOSC) that I also chaired for two years. Loek and I developed the Open Science Community Incubator Program, a free 14-weeks online program to help people around the world to set up, run, and maintain their own local OSC. I love to share my experiences with Open Science and running a community to help and inspire others. I also have a podcast (in Dutch) on psychology (Drang naar Samenhang) together with Rolf Zwaan, and in my free time I like to cook, bake, hike, read, and solve puzzles (from old school jigsaws to escape rooms).
